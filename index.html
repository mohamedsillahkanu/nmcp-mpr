Overview
Malaria Program Review (MPR) for the National Malaria Control Programme
The Malaria Program Review (MPR) is a comprehensive, country-led assessment designed to evaluate the performance, relevance, and impact of all aspects of a national malaria response. For the National Malaria Control Programme (NMCP) in Sierra Leone, the MPR is a critical opportunity to reflect on progress, identify gaps, and re-align priorities with the evolving malaria epidemiology, global best practices, and national health goals.
Purpose of the MPR
The main purpose of the MPR is to ensure that the malaria program remains efficient, effective, and results-oriented. It supports evidence-based planning and strengthens program accountability, coordination, and resource allocation. The MPR also prepares the groundwork for the development of new strategic plans and funding proposals to partners such as the Global Fund and PMI.

Relevance of School-Based ITN Distribution in the MPR
The School-Based Distribution (SBD) of Insecticide-Treated Nets (ITNs) is a promising intervention that addresses the challenge of maintaining universal ITN coverage between mass campaigns. Its integration into the MPR highlights considerations such as:

â€¢ Partnerships: Operational collaboration between health and education sectors.
â€¢ Outcomes: Usage rates, knowledge of ITN care, and its impact on malaria morbidity among school-age children.

The Malaria Program Review is not just a routine assessment; it is a strategic pivot point for NMCP to enhance impact, secure sustained funding, and ultimately achieve malaria elimination goals. With interventions like the School-Based ITN Distribution gaining traction, Sierra Leone is well-positioned to redefine its malaria control architecture with a stronger emphasis on integration, innovation, and inclusiveness.
